Geological Aspect Points



Tyrannosaurus rex (T. rex)
� Time Period: Late Cretaceous (68�66 million years ago).
� Location: Western North America (Montana, South Dakota, Wyoming).
� Habitat: Floodplains, forests, and coastal regions.
� Diet: Carnivore; apex predator feeding on herbivorous dinosaurs like Triceratops.
� Features: Massive size (~12 meters long), powerful jaws, excellent sense of smell.
� Significance: One of the largest land predators of all time.


. Triceratops
� Time Period: Late Cretaceous (68�66 million years ago).
� Location: Western North America (Montana, South Dakota, Wyoming).
� Habitat: Plains, floodplains, and forests.
� Diet: Herbivore; ate ferns, cycads, and low-lying plants.
� Features: Three facial horns, large frill, massive body (~9 meters long).
� Significance: Iconic herbivore, coexisted with T. rex, known for its defensive horns.


Apatosaurus
� Time Period: Late Jurassic (152�151 million years ago).
� Location: Western North America (Colorado, Utah, Wyoming).
� Habitat: Floodplains, river valleys, and forests.
� Diet: Herbivore; fed on conifers, cycads, and ferns.
� Features: Long neck (~21 meters long overall), whip-like tail, large size (~20�25 tons).
� Significance: A classic example of a giant sauropod dinosaur.
