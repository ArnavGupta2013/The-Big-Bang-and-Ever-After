The Debate of What Came after Big Bang In Order



1. The First Life (~3.8 Billion Years Ago)
The earliest life forms were microbes (single-celled organisms) in Earth's oceans.
These simple bacteria-like organisms existed for billions of years before complex life appeared.


2. The First Animals (~600 Million Years Ago)
Soft-bodied creatures like sponges and jellyfish evolved.
Later, worms, arthropods, and fish appeared.


3. The First Land Animals (~360 Million Years Ago)
Some fish evolved legs and became amphibians, moving onto land.
Later, reptiles appeared, leading to both dinosaurs and mammals.


4. Dinosaurs Came First! (~230 Million Years Ago)
Dinosaurs evolved in the Triassic Period (~230 MYA) and ruled for 165 million years.
They dominated the Earth until an asteroid wiped them out 66 million years ago.


5. Mammals Came Later! (~225 Million Years Ago)
Small, shrew-like mammals appeared around the same time as dinosaurs (~225 MYA).
But they stayed small and hid from dinosaurs.
After the dinosaurs went extinct, mammals evolved into bigger animals like elephants, whales, and eventually humans.
