Science Debates



1. Tyrannosaurus rex (T. rex) – "The King of Dinosaurs"
Predator or Scavenger Debate

T. rex had a strong bite force (over 12,000 pounds of pressure!) and sharp teeth, suggesting it was a top predator.
However, some scientists believe it was also a scavenger, feeding on dead animals.
Recent evidence shows it likely did both (like modern lions or hyenas).

Speed & Movement
Earlier, scientists thought T. rex was very fast.
Modern research suggests it was not a sprinter but could walk fast (~10-15 mph).
Its long tail helped with balance.

Feathers or Scales?
Fossils suggest T. rex hatchlings may have had feathers, but adults likely had mostly scaly skin with some feathered patches.


2. Triceratops – "The Horned Herbivore"
Horns for Defense or Display?
The three horns and frill were once thought to be for fighting predators (like T. rex).
Some scientists believe they were also used for mating displays (like a peacock’s feathers).
Social Behavior
Fossil evidence suggests Triceratops may have lived alone or in small groups, unlike other ceratopsians that lived in herds.

Beak & Diet
It had a sharp beak for cutting plants and rows of teeth that constantly grew back.
It mainly ate ferns, palms, and cycads.


3. Apatosaurus – "The Long Herbivore"
Neck Position Debate
Earlier, scientists thought Apatosaurus held its neck high like a giraffe.
Newer research suggests it kept its neck horizontal to eat low vegetation.

Tail as a Whip
Apatosaurus had a long, whip-like tail, which may have been used to make loud cracking sounds to scare predators.

Warm-blooded or Cold-blooded?
Some scientists think large dinosaurs like Apatosaurus were warm-blooded (endothermic) to keep their massive bodies active.
Others argue they were mesothermic (somewhere between warm- and cold-blooded).
