When AND Where AND Whom



1. Tyrannosaurus rex (T. rex)
First Discovery: 1902 Location: Hell Creek Formation, Montana, USA.
Discovered By: Barnum Brown, a paleontologist working for the American Museum of Natural History. Significant Fossils:
"Sue": Found in 1990 by Sue Hendrickson in South Dakota, the most complete T. rex skeleton.
"Stan": Found in 1987 by Stan Sacrison in South Dakota, another well-preserved specimen.


2. Apatosaurus
First Discovery: 1877 Location: Morrison Formation, Colorado, USA
. Discovered By: Othniel Charles Marsh during the famous �Bone Wars,� a period of intense fossil competition.
Significant Fossils: Early fossils were misidentified as belonging to different genera, including Brontosaurus (later corrected)..


3. Triceratops
First Discovery: 1887 Location: Denver Formation, Colorado, USA.
Discovered By: Initially identified by George Lyman Cannon, though misclassified as a bison.
Later properly described by Othniel Charles Marsh in 1889. Significant Fossils: Numerous specimens found in the Hell Creek Formation, Montana, and South Dakota.
"Big John": The largest Triceratops skeleton, discovered in South Dakota in 2014.
